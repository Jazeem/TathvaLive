Do not expect mind-boggling questions and unbreakable tasks at Pure Trics for all challenges here are meant to test your very basics and rational approach to the boundless field of electrical technology.

EVENT SPECIFICATION

ROUND 1
  • This round will be a general and electrical aptitude MCQ round. Top 10 teams will be qualified to the second round.
ROUND 2
  • The second round will be purely electrical aptitude and will be testing the deep knowledge in basics of electrical science. For this, each team will be given 10 questions one after another. Only after answering/skipping a question, a team will be given the next question. If they answer correctly, 5 points will be awarded, no points will be awarded for a skipped question and -1 will be the penalty for a wrong answer. If any team is stuck in a question, they can ask for a clue. But clue will have a penalty of -2 points. So there are six ways of scoring:
  1. Answering correctly without a clue : 5 points
  2. Skipping : 0 points
  3. Wrong answer : -1 point
  4. Answering correctly with clue : 3 points
  5. Skipping after asking a clue : -2 points
  6. Wrong answer after asking clue : -3 points
  • A team cannot skip more than 3 questions. When a team completes all the 10 questions, the round will be over. The overall marks of each team will then be calculated and the top 5 teams will be qualified to the final round.
FINAL ROUND
  • This round is a hands-on round where a problem statement and equipments will be provided for each team using which the solution should be implemented as an electrical circuit. 50 points will be awarded for the winners of this round ,30 for runner ups and 20 for 2nd runner ups.

TEAM SPECIFICATION

  • A team can have a minimum of two members and maximum of three.
  • Cross college teams are permitted.
  • Participants from any branch of engineering have the liberty to take part.

RULES AND REGULATIONS

  • Teams are NOT entitled to carry personal components to the event. All the components required for the event will be provided by the organisers.
  • For cross college teams,points will be equally distributed among the colleges.
  • In case of any malpractice teams will be disqualified.

    • JUDGING CRITERIA

    • Round 1 will be having a minimum cut off and teams scoring above that will qualify to round 2.
    • Top 5 teams of Round 2 will be qualified to the final round.
    • In the final round, simplicity of the logic, minimal use of resources, neatness of the circuit and time taken to complete the task will be considered for judgement.
    • Final score will be the sum of Round 2 marks and Round 3 marks.Winners will be decided by the total score.
    • In case of any discrepancy, the decision of the event coordinators shall be final in all respects.
