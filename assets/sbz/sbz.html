INTRODUCTION

For Gen-Y, the line which separates “Entrepreneur” from “Social Entrepreneur” is fast fading. We are a generation of change makers and innovators. This event is for all the dreamers who have the courage to defy convention and transform tomorrow. Let's be a part of a change through Sociobizz'13, a Social B-plan competition aimed at creating sustainable and commercially viable business plans to the most challenging social problems.

SPECIFICATIONS

The event shall be conducted in two rounds:
Round 1: EXECUTIVE SUMMARY ROUND
  1. In this round participants will be submitting the executive summary of their social B-plan to the following email: sociobizz13@tathva.org.
    • The summary must be submitted in PDF (Standard Online Publishing and Printing) format.
    • The summary must be typed in 12pt Times New Roman font,1.5pt line space and 1-inch margins on all sides inclusive of text, charts, and diagrams.
    • The summary should not exceed 3 pages excluding the cover page.
    • The cover page of the abstract should contain the following details:
      • NAME OF TEAM MEMBERS:
      EDUCATIONAL INSTITUTE:
      CONTACT DETAILS: (E-mail and contact number of any 2 members).
    • The filename should be titled using the following format: TeamName_Sociobizz_ExecSummary.pdf
  2. The abstract should clearly outline your Social Business idea. Financials may be provided if necessary.
  3. Based upon the abstract teams will be short-listed for the for the final round.
  4. The short listed teams will receive intensive mentoring from our distinguished panel of members.

    5. JUDGING CRITERIA

    Social B-plans will be evaluated on these 4 criteria:

    1. Technological innovation specifically addressing the quality of life for the poor.
    2. New or innovative use of an existing technology that exceeds previous or current solutions.
    3. Business idea that serves as a model or best practice for applying technology or has the potential to be scaled.
    4. Rigor and feasibility of the financial model including sustainability over long term.

    Plans must clearly demonstrate the Social Return on Investment (SROI) and provide a social impact assessment.

    RULES AND REGULATIONS

    • Last date for submission of abstract is 10th October 2013.
    • The business plans may focus on any subject area.
    • The competition is open to students currently enrolled in any disciple or level of study, at any academic institution worldwide.
    • Multidisciplinary teams are strongly encouraged.
    • Participant should be registered for Tathva'13 and thereby, produce a valid Tathva'13 ID at the time of registration/event.
    • The participant should abide by all the decisions taken by the judging panel and the Tycoon Tathva'13 Organising Committee.
    • The participant should maintain discipline at all times and follow proper code of conduct failing which his/her registration might be cancelled at any instant of time.
