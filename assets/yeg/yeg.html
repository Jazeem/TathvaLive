Project Expo

The event will take place in the form of two rounds:
Screening round
  • Participants are required to submit a brief description of their project online.
  • Images and videos of the projects are encouraged.
  • Among the projects, a preliminary evaluation will be done by the organizers.
  • Top 40 teams will be selected for the final round.
Exhibition
  • The selected teams are to exhibit their projects during Tathva.
  • It will be in the form of a public exhibition wherein the participants would have to describe their projects to the public as well as the judges.
  • The judges would finally evaluate them and select 3 winning projects.
  • Recommendations and feedback from the organizers would be provided for as many projects as possible.

Engineering Aptitude

This section will consist of 2 rounds as described below:

Preliminary round
  • Written round, in a questionnaire format, will be conducted at various schools.
  • The participants should form groups of 2 and attempt the questions.
  • Questions will be more about observations and knowledge pertaining to the engineering discipline rather than a formal test of engineering aptitude.
  • The preliminary round is open to all.
  • The top 20 finalists will be notified through email.

Hands-on round
  • The second round comprises of a series of practical problems to be attempted by the teams at Tathva.
  • The tasks would involve knowledge about the basic concepts of engineering.
  • The problem statement will not be given beforehand.
  • However, the participants will be introduced to a variety of concepts and other tutorials, through email.
  • Top 3 teams will be declared as winners of the event and awarded prizes.

Judging Criteria

PROJECT EXHIBTION
The evaluation will take into account the following points:
  • Innovation
  • Benefit to society
  • Simplicity of design
  • Cost of production/implementation
The evaluation pattern pertains to both preliminary as well as final round

ENGINEERING APTITUDE
Points would be awarded based on:
  • The efficiency of the solution.
  • The time taken to solve the problem.
  • Specific criteria pertaining to the tasks.
  • Innovation and creativity.
  • Engineering ingenuity shown in the presented solution.
