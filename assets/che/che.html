INTRODUCTION

At the start of the 20th century, Henry Ford planned to fuel his Model Ts with ethanol. Almost at the same time, one morning in 1935, The Dallas Morning News reported a demonstration of a water fuelled car by Charles H.Garrett. Attempts have always been made to run vehicles on chemical reactions all around the world.

Sir Henry Royce said "Strive for perfection in everything you do. Take the best that exists and make it better. When it does not exist, design it." At Tathva 13 gear up your roadsters against the best techies across India, for an ultimate test of endurance, to leave your opponents baffled in the heat of the reactions.

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Design and fabricate a self-propelled car which runs on a controlled chemical reaction and completes the specified track in minimum time.

SPECIFICATIONS

General Specifications
1. Maximum of four members per team. Cross college teams are allowed.
2. Team members should produce a valid ID card of their institution.
3. Dimensions: The Car should fit into a box of size 30cm x 25cm x 20cm.
4. Ready-made kits should not be used for the event (eg. LEGO).
5. No commercial batteries, motors can be used. Motors if used must derive it's power from a chemical reaction.
6. Use of hazardous chemicals like dynamite, fireworks, mercuric chloride, white phosphorous, nitroglycerin are strictly forbidden.
7. Cellulose, Aluminium alkyls, gunpowder and other explosive chemicals should not be used for the propulsion.
8. Poisonous gases like Hydrogen Cyanide, fluorine and chlorine etc should not be emitted from the machine during the event.
9. The car must not damage the track. If it does, the team will be disqualified.
10. Teams may be given a second chance and/or re-try after the initial rounds based on the competition and necessity. The decision of the judges will be final and binding to all.
11. Decision of judges and coordinators is final and all the teams shall abide by it.
12.Every component of the car will be subject to examination and a small abstract on the working of the car should be submitted at the time of the event.
Event Format
1. Each team will be given three rounds for running their car on the track and points will be given separately for each round.
2. The best performance out of the three would be considered for final evaluation.

ARENA SPECIFICATIONS

1. The track will be straight with different turfs of sand, wet area (water till tire level only; floating mechanism is not required), lubrication(wax) and inclinations.
2. The length of the track will be 6m.
3. There will be walls along both the edges of the track (only one wall is shown in the model track for convenience).
4. The model track is only a probable estimate of the actual track, uploaded for the ease of the participants. Actual track may vary slightly from the model track.

JUDGING CRITERIA

1. The car which reaches the end point in the shortest time wins.
2. The car must touch all surfaces of the track, if not points will be given according to the number of surfaces the car touches.
3. If two cars reach the end point in the same time, points will be given to the innovation of the chemical reaction used.The weight of the car will also be a criteria for judging.The lighter car gains more points.
4. The final decision lies with the judges.
