INTRODUCTION

Why limit yourself to the rules and laws of the already established when imagination can make you fly?
If you think your idea could be a solution to a real world problem within the realms of science and technology –Folks, the stage is all set!
Blueprint is a core paper presentation event, where the best scientific minds come, compete, learn and share their ideas.

Rules

Rules
• There can be a maximum of two members per team.
• Team members must register online for the event. On registering, members will be mailed a unique Tathva '13 ID which shall be useful during the course of the event and while sending the abstract.(In case the registrations have not started, keep visiting the site and register when it's up.)
• For screening purposes, a pre-event abstract, along with your Tathva IDs and other details has to be submitted in .pdf or .doc format. Abstract, of maximum 1000 words, should include a brief introduction, motivation, methodology adopted, results obtained and practical application. The abstract should be mailed to blueprint@tathva.org.
• In the pre-event abstract to be sent, the participant should mention as to under which department, under which topic he/she wishes to present the paper.
• The last date for the submission of pre-event abstracts will be 10th October 2013.
• Short listed teams will be informed by mail. The list of short listed teams will also be displayed on the Tathva '13 website.
• Selected participants will be given 20 minutes to present their paper before a panel of judges, of which 15 minutes will be for presentation and 5 minutes for the discussion.
• The participant is expected to bring 3 hard copies as well as a soft copy of the paper.
• Along with the pre-event abstract, we request the participants to send a short write up (250 – 300 words) on how they came aross the idea and the motivation behind it. The practical application of the research, citing places and including local incidents with relevant proof has a different prize and shall not be taken as a criterion for scrutiny.
