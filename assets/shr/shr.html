Event Format

The event consists of two stages. The first stage is the online submission of the abstract in the prescribed format. A final list of participants will then be shortlisted and they will receive a confirmation email. In the final stage participants are required to present their ideas using ppt preferably with working models, supplemented by a printed report. The presentation time will be 30 minutes.

The abstract should contain a:
  • Brief and specific description of the social problem identified.
  • Brief description about the working solution designed to solve the problem.

Rules

  • Interested teams should sent their abstract to srishti@tathva.org on or before 5th October 2013.
  • There WILL NOT be any prelim rounds for this event, the entries are directly evaluated and shortlisted participants are allowed to participate in the finals.
  • A team may be represented by a maximum of 4 members.
  • Accommodation for the participants will be arranged if informed well in advance, and subject to availability.
  • Selected contestants will get a chance to associate with NGOs to implement their models at the mass level, subject to the terms and conditions of respective NGOs.
  • At every stage of the competition the decision of the panel members and the organizers will be final.
  • The dates and deadlines may change, and on such an incident, it shall be conveyed to all the registered participants
  • The Intellectual Property of the project and further works shall be in public domain, once the user submits his project.
  • It shall be the participant's responsibility to ensure that there are no intellectual property violations, and the institute shall not be responsible for the same.

Judging Criteria

  • Ideas will be judged by an expert panel.
  • The panel will look for cost-effective, feasible working models which can make a difference to the society.
  • Cost to utility aspect will be given preference during judging.
