Do you think you know enough about aerodynamics? Do you feel you can make and fly a paper aircraft that can clear the world record of 226 feet and 10 inches? Well here is the opportunity all of you were waiting for - Aerogami.

A fun event for students who are skilled and creative in the field of aerodynamics, the event challenges the students to make paper airplanes using a simple A-4 sheet and let it fly over the largest possible distance.

INTRODUCTION

Problem statement

Participants are given the task of flying their paper aircraft to the maximum possible distance within the walls. The paper aircraft can only be folded.

Judging criterion

The team covering the maximum distance in a single throw will be the winners.

Rules

• Given paper sheets can only be folded. Ripping, gluing, stapling, taping or ballasting of the given sheet is not permitted.
• Scale cannot be used for folding.
• Participants can only use the paper provided at the venue.
• Total of three A-4 sheets will be given to each team.
• Each team will be given a total of 15 minutes to prepare their designs.
• Each team will be given two attempts in total.
• The distance achieved on each attempt will be noted.
• The aircraft must not go behind the launch point and must stay within the walls of the room.
• The paper aircraft must be launched by one person throwing the aircraft, unaided, from a reasonably static position within the marked area (run ups are not allowed).
• The best of the two throws will be taken into account.
• Decision of the event managers will be final.
